GET ACTIVE

Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan at the YMCA in Holyoke, Massachusetts. Originally called "Mintonette", it was designed as a less physically demanding alternative to basketball. Today it is one of the most played sports in the world, with over 800 million players in 220 countries.

Olympic debut in Tokyo

Section 02A · Theory Block

Rules & Court

Volleyball is played on an 18m x 9m court divided by a net. Each team has a maximum of 3 contacts to return the ball. Understanding the core rules is the foundation of the game.

3
Maximum contacts
Each team has 3 touches to return the ball. The same player cannot hit it twice consecutively.
Fundamental rule
🔄
Rotation
After winning a rally while the opponent served (side-out), players rotate clockwise.
Mandatory rotation
📏
Court zones
Front zone (attack): 3 attackers near the net. Back zone (defence): 3 defenders at the baseline.
Two zones
🏅
Rally point
A point is scored on every rally regardless of which team served — modern volleyball.
Scoring system

Section 02B · Theory Block

The Six Positions

Each position in volleyball has a specific role in both attack and defence. Understanding these roles is essential for effective team play.

1
Outside Hitter
Main attacker from the left side. Most versatile player — attacks, serves and passes.
Primary attacker
2
Setter
The playmaker. Touches the ball on the 2nd contact and delivers accurate sets to attackers.
Team architect
3
Libero
Defensive specialist in a different colour jersey. Cannot attack, block or serve.
Defensive specialist
4
Middle Blocker
Tallest player. Blocks at the net and performs quick attacks through the middle zone.
Net dominance

Section 02C · Theory Block

Offensive Techniques

Volleyball offence flows through three linked skills: serve, set and spike. Each requires specific technique.

🤜
Float Serve
No spin. The ball moves unpredictably in the air — the most effective serve in modern volleyball.
Unpredictable movement
🙌
Overhead Set
Fingertip contact to direct the ball accurately to the attacker. The setter's primary weapon.
Precision required
💥
Spike
Jumping and hitting the ball downward with power and angle into the opponent's court.
Point winner
⚡
Jump Serve
Tossed high, approached and hit like a spike from behind the end line. Maximum speed.
Power serve

Section 02D · Theory Block

Defensive Techniques

Great defence is what separates good teams from great teams. The forearm pass, block and dive are the foundations of defensive volleyball.

🫰
Forearm Pass (Dig)
Two forearms together forming a flat platform to redirect a hard-driven ball upward.
Defensive foundation
🤚
Block
Jumping at the net with arms extended to deflect the opponent's attack. 1, 2 or 3 players.
First line of defence
🤸
Dive / Roll
Emergency technique to save a ball close to the ground without losing court position.
Emergency save
📡
Court Coverage
Defenders must read the setter, attacker and block to position correctly before the attack.
Anticipation

Section 02E · Theory Block

Team Systems & Game Tactics

Volleyball teams use specific offensive and defensive systems to maximise their strengths and exploit opponents' weaknesses.

6-2
6-2 System
6 attackers, 2 setters (one always in back row). More attacking options but complex rotation.
Complex offence
5-1
5-1 System
One setter — the most common at high level. Consistent setting but fewer attacking options.
Standard system
4-2
4-2 System
4 attackers, 2 setters (always in front row). Simpler for beginners and school teams.
Beginner-friendly
🔄
Rotation Systems
Serve receive patterns and rotation position assignments that keep the best attackers in the right spots.
Tactical positioning

Section 02F · Theory Block

Beach Volleyball & Physical Fitness

Beach volleyball (2 vs 2 on sand) became Olympic in 1996. It develops the same skills as indoor but with added physical demands from the sand surface.

🏖
Beach vs Indoor
2 players instead of 6. Same skills, different physical demands. No libero. Players cover the full court.
Key difference
💪
Sand Training
Playing on sand increases lower-body muscular demand — jumps are 15-20% harder than on hard court.
Physical advantage
❤️
Fitness Benefits
Volleyball develops cardiovascular endurance, explosive power, coordination and reaction time.
Full fitness package
🧠
Reaction Time
Serving, passing and attacking require split-second decisions at high ball speeds.
Neurological demand

Section 03 · Train & Compete

TRAIN & COMPETE

Complete these challenges and record your results. Focus on technique before speed or power.

01
Forearm Pass Wall Rally
Technical

Stand 2 metres from a wall and dig the ball continuously using forearm passes at head height. Count the most consecutive contacts without losing control.

02
Setting Accuracy
Technical

Stand 2 metres from a wall and set the ball to a target mark at 2.5 metres height. How many out of 10 hit the target? Try with both hands.

03
Serve Placement
Tactical

Attempt 10 float serves. Target each corner of the opponent's court. Count how many land in the target zone vs random placement.

04
3-Person Passing Circuit
Team

With 2 teammates, practice the serve-receive sequence: one player serves, one passes to the third who sets and the passer spikes. Rotate after 10 rallies.

05
Mini-game (3 vs 3)
Game

Play a 3 vs 3 game (modified rules if needed) applying at least 3 tactical concepts from this unit. Reflect on your decision-making during the game.
