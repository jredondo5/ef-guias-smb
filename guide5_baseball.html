GET ACTIVE

Baseball evolved from the English game "rounders" in the early 19th century. By the late 1800s it had become deeply embedded in American culture. Today, baseball is wildly popular throughout Latin America, Japan, South Korea and Cuba. The sport is increasingly seen in European school PE programmes for its unique combination of individual skill, team strategy and mathematical thinking — no other sport uses statistics like baseball.

1869
First professional team
The Cincinnati Red Stockings became the first professional baseball team in 1869, marking the start of the professional era.
1947
Jackie Robinson breaks the color barrier
One of sport's most significant civil rights moments — Robinson became the first Black player in Major League Baseball.
27.4m
Distance between bases
90 feet (27.4 metres) — one of the game's iconic numbers, perfectly balanced for infield defence.

History & Cultural Impact

Baseball's history is inseparable from American social and cultural history. The sport has been a mirror of society — including its darkest chapters.

📜
1845 Rules
Alexander Cartwright wrote the first standardised baseball rules — the foundation of the modern game.
Birth of modern baseball
🏆
1903 World Series
The first World Series was played in 1903 between the Pittsburgh Pirates and Boston Americans.
Baseball's championship
✊
Jackie Robinson (1947)
Robinson broke the Major League Baseball color barrier — one of sport's most significant civil rights moments.
Civil rights landmark
🌍
Global game
Dominican Republic, Venezuela, Cuba, Japan and South Korea are now major baseball powers.
Beyond America

Section 02B · Theory Block

The Diamond & Rules

The baseball diamond — four bases arranged in a square — is the defining symbol of the sport. Understanding how runs are scored and outs are made is the foundation of the game.

💎
The Diamond
Four bases (home, 1st, 2nd, 3rd) arranged in a square. 27.4m between each base.
The playing field
⚾
Innings
9 innings per game. Each team bats and fields once per inning. 3 outs end each half-inning.
Game structure
🎯
Scoring
A runner scores when they touch all four bases and return to home plate without being put out.
How runs are scored
❌
Three ways to make an out
Strikeout (3 strikes), fly out (caught in the air), ground out (thrown to base before runner arrives).
How outs are made

The Nine Positions

Baseball has nine fielding positions, each with specific responsibilities. Understanding roles on the field is essential for both batting and fielding strategy.

1
Pitcher
Most important defensive player. Controls tempo, throws to strikeout batters.
Game control
2
Catcher
Crouches behind home plate. Calls pitches, manages defence, throws out base stealers.
Field general
6
Shortstop
Most versatile infielder. Covers 2nd-3rd base gap. Often the best defensive player.
Defensive anchor
8
Centre Field
Covers the largest outfield zone. Usually the fastest outfielder — covers the most ground.
Outfield leader

Section 02D · Theory Block

Batting & Pitching Technique

Hitting a baseball thrown at 150+ km/h requires extraordinary timing and technique. Pitching requires both physical skill and psychological strategy.

🏏
Batting Stance
Balanced, slightly crouched, weight on the balls of the feet, bat held comfortably above the shoulder.
The foundation
🔄
Hip Rotation
The power in a swing comes from explosive hip rotation — not arm strength. Hips lead the hands.
Power source
⚾
Fastball
The most common pitch. 4-seam grip for maximum speed and straight trajectory.
Primary pitch
🌀
Breaking Ball
Curveball, slider or sinker — each with different spin creating different movement to deceive the batter.
Deception pitches

Section 02E · Theory Block

Fielding & Base Running

Defence wins championships. Fielding and baserunning are the most overlooked but critically important aspects of baseball strategy.

🫱
Fielding Position
Weight forward, glove down and open, ready to move in any direction when the ball is hit.
Ready position
🏃
Base Running
Reading the pitcher, knowing the situation, and making smart decisions about when to run.
Strategic running
🤾
Double Play
Two outs on one play — the most exciting defensive play. Shortstop or 2nd baseman pivots and throws.
Defence's best play
🎯
Throwing Mechanics
Four-seam grip, step toward target, throw from over the top for accuracy and speed.
Arm action

Section 02F · Theory Block

Baseball Analytics & Physical Demands

Baseball was the pioneering sport in analytics — using statistics to make better decisions. The physical demands of the sport are also highly specific.

📊
Batting Average
Hits divided by at-bats. The traditional measure of a batter's effectiveness — though increasingly complemented by OPS, WAR.
Classic stat
📈
Moneyball
The Oakland A's 2002 use of advanced stats (Sabermetrics) to build a competitive team on a small budget — revolutionised sport.
Analytics revolution
💪
Physical Demands
Explosive power (pitching, batting), rotational strength (swing), arm endurance (fielding) and sprint speed.
Athletic requirements
🧠
Decision Making
Baseball players make split-second decisions 100+ times per game — reading pitches, reads, defensive shifts.
Cognitive demands

Section 03 · Train & Compete

TRAIN & COMPETE

Complete these challenges. Record your results and upload evidence to Classroom.

01
Batting Tee Practice
Technique

Using a batting tee (or improvised equivalent), focus on hip rotation. Record 20 swings analysing: contact point, hip turn and follow-through. Film yourself from the side.

02
Pitching Mechanics
Arm action

Using a rubber ball against a wall target (or with a partner), practise your four-seam fastball mechanics. Focus on stride length and arm path. 30 throws minimum.

03
Fielding Ground Balls
Fielding

With a partner rolling balls to you, field 20 ground balls: 10 directly at you, 5 to your right, 5 to your left. Focus on the ready position and glove angle.

04
Baserunning Circuit
Speed & Strategy

Set up a simple 3-base circuit (or use PE cones). Time yourself running through first base, rounding second and stopping at third. Repeat 5 times with sprint.

05
Mini-game (Modified Baseball)
Game

Play a modified baseball game with your class. Focus on applying: correct fielding position, hip rotation in batting, and running through first base.
