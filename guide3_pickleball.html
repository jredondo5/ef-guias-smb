History & Origins

Pickleball was invented in 1965 on Bainbridge Island, Washington. Three fathers — Joel Pritchard, Bill Bell and Barney McCallum — created it to entertain their families using improvised equipment from their garage.

📅
1965
Year of invention on Bainbridge Island, Washington by three fathers.
Origin
🏓
Hybrid Sport
Combines elements of tennis (court size, scoring), badminton (net height) and ping-pong (paddle feel).
Three sports in one
🇺🇸
US Growth
In 2023, the USA Pickleball Association reported over 8.9 million players — a 158% increase in 3 years.
Fastest growing sport
🇪🇸
Spain
The Royal Spanish Tennis Federation officially integrated pickleball in 2020 — growing in schools and clubs.
European expansion

Section 02B · Theory Block

Court, Equipment & Scoring

The pickleball court is the same size as a doubles badminton court. Understanding the key zones — especially the kitchen — is essential for playing correctly.

📏
Court Size
20ft x 44ft (6.1m x 13.4m). Same for singles and doubles. Net height: 91cm at sidelines, 86cm in the centre.
Dimensions
🍳
The Kitchen
The non-volley zone (NVZ) — 7ft either side of the net. Cannot volley while standing in or touching the kitchen line.
Most important zone
🏓
Equipment
Solid paddle (wood, composite or graphite) and a perforated plastic ball. Different balls for indoor and outdoor.
Gear
🔢
Scoring
First to 11 points (win by 2). Only the serving team scores. Tournament play to 15 or 21.
Points system

Section 02C · Theory Block

Core Rules: Double Bounce & Kitchen

Two rules make pickleball unique among racket sports: the double bounce rule and the non-volley zone (kitchen) rule. Understanding them is essential for correct play.

2x
Double Bounce Rule
The ball must bounce once on each side before either team can volley. This prevents serve-and-volley dominance.
Eliminates power serves
🚫
Kitchen Rule
No volleying while in the NVZ. You may enter the kitchen after the ball bounces — but not to volley.
Strategy changer
↗️
Serve Rules
Underhand serve below the waist, diagonally cross-court. Must clear the kitchen and land in the opposite service box.
Serve constraints
🔄
Side-Out
When the serving team faults, service passes to the opponent. In doubles, both partners serve before side-out.
Service rotation

Section 02D · Theory Block

The Five Key Shots

Pickleball rewards placement and strategy over power. These five shots form the technical foundation every player must develop.

↗️
Serve
Underhand, below waist. Aim deep to push opponent back. Consistency trumps power.
Initiate the rally
↩️
Return
Drive it deep — keep the serving team at the baseline — then rush to the kitchen line.
Tactical positioning
🫧
Dink
Soft shot arcing gently over the net, landing in opponent's kitchen. The signature shot of pickleball.
The most strategic shot
💥
Volley
Hit out of the air before it bounces. Must be outside the kitchen. Speed and angle.
Attacking shot
🎯
Third Shot Drop
The most important tactical shot — played softly on shot 3 to allow the serving team to advance to the net.
Key transition shot

Section 02E · Theory Block

Doubles Tactics & Positioning

Doubles is by far the most popular format in pickleball. Understanding positioning and movement patterns is the key to winning rallies.

🏆
Kitchen Line Dominance
Control the kitchen line and you control the rally. Both partners should move to the NVZ as fast as possible.
Positioning priority
🤝
Move Together
Doubles partners move as a unit — side by side, mirroring each other. Never let a large gap open.
Coordinate movement
🎯
Stacking
An advanced tactic where both players position on the same side to exploit a strong forehand.
Advanced formation
🧠
Shot Selection
In a dinking rally, look for a "speedup" opportunity — attack a high ball to force an error.
Reading the rally

Section 02F · Theory Block

Pickleball & Physical Fitness

Pickleball provides a complete physical workout while being low impact and accessible to all ages and fitness levels.

❤️
Cardiovascular
Rallies require constant movement — stepping, lunging, pivoting. Excellent aerobic workout.
Heart health
💪
Muscular
Engages core, legs and arms simultaneously. Lateral movement strengthens stabiliser muscles.
Full body
⚖️
Balance & Coordination
Constant direction changes and eye-hand coordination development.
Neuromuscular benefits
🧠
Cognitive
Strategy, anticipation and decision-making under pressure — pickleball is chess at speed.
Mental workout

Section 03 · Train & Compete

TRAIN & COMPETE

Complete these challenges and record your results. Upload evidence to Classroom.

01
Serve Accuracy Test
Technical

Attempt 10 underhand serves from the correct position. Count how many land in the target service box. Try to beat your score in a second attempt after practising.

02
Dink Rally
Consistency

With a partner, stand at the kitchen line and maintain a continuous dinking rally. Count your longest streak without an error. Can you beat 20 consecutive dinks?

03
Third Shot Drop Practice
Tactical shot

From the baseline, attempt 10 third shot drops aiming to land softly in the opponent's kitchen. Your partner feeds you the second shot from the net.

04
Mini-Game (Dinks Only)
Game application

Play a point-scoring game with a partner where all shots must bounce in the kitchen or be a dink — no hard drives allowed. First to 11 wins.

05
Full Game Match
Competitive play

Play a full game to 11 (win by 2) against a classmate applying all the rules learned. Reflect on your tactical decision-making.
